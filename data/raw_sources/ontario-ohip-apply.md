The official Ontario page explains that the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP) pays for many health services people may need. It says people need to apply and, once approved, receive an Ontario health card that shows they are covered by OHIP.

The page includes information about health care in Ontario, what OHIP may cover, who may qualify, how to apply, reapplying, OHIP for babies, adopted children, military family members, visitors to Ontario, and requesting a review.

The page says OHIP applications are made in person at a ServiceOntario centre and that applicants need to bring a completed registration form and separate documents for status, Ontario residency, and identity.

Do not use this summary to decide eligibility. The exact eligibility rules, document requirements, exceptions, and appeal deadlines must be read from the official page.
